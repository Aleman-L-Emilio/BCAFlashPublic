BCA Flash 2022 Courses

Aviation Fundamentals

Ever wonder about the physics and function of planes, or have an interest in learning more about aviation? This course will cover modern design and controls of planes to the physics that make them work. From economic to technological strategies, this class will also introduce you to all you need to know about the incredible growth of the aviation industry, and all the parts that allow it to function efficiently.

The Art of Pastry: Nutella Croissants

Our class focuses on pastry by demonstrating the process through the making of nutella croissants. We used frozen puff pastry and created this concept based on minimal ingredients and materials so that anyone can follow along. We began our class by describing food/kitchen safety tips, and continued by describing terms, ingredients, and materials. We then executed the procedure, and concluded with an explanation of lamination, which is a process that is created by the usage of fat folded into dough. We have both a video resource and a slideshow for reference. We hope the students enjoy this class and learn about the art of pastry!

Model United Nations

There's black tea, there's bubble tea, but most importantly, there's international security! Model United Nations is a simulation of the United Nations General Assembly and other international multilateral bodies where students represent an ambassador from a nation to debate on topics ranging from the Ethics of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles to Illicit Trade to Environmental Concerns. In this Model UN class, you will learn about the intricate dynamics of international relations in the United Nations and how you can apply them in Model UN. We will go over parliamentary procedure, speech writing, and powerful speech styles to master the nuances of negotiation. Using these skills, you can compete in prestigious conferences and simulate discussions of solutions to world issues.

Skincare Science

In this lesson and virtual lab, students will learn about skincare products and how their ingredients work to make your skin look clean and clear--just like in the advertisements. We'll take a closer look at the interactions between the products and your skin, and how those interactions work together to make visible changes to your body's biggest organ. In particular, we will dive deeper into the role of pH in the chemistry behind some of our favorite skincare products. From indicator solutions to litmus paper, we will discuss the colorful ways pH can be measured. Finally, we will tie it all back to the role of active and inactive ingredients in performing the product's aesthetic function and protecting the biological makeup behind your skin.

Neuroscience: Anxiety vs. Anxiety Disorders

Everybody gets stressed, but does everybody get anxiety? Does everybody get anxiety disorders? Yes, the answer might be psychological, but in this class, we'll be explaining the SCIENCE BEHIND ANXIETY and what is scientifically classified as anxiety versus anxiety disorders. By Sophia Jiang, Karis Kim, Esther Kim, Mariam Ali, and Hooriya Bagasra

Universe of the Zentangle

The zentangle is a method of relaxing through art with by using patterns of shapes. It can be easily created by using a pen and a paper. We will introduce the history of the zentangle with some of the examples, and actually get into the drawing of Zentangle! Through our lesson, we would hope to teach the lesson that it is okay to imagine big and how those ideas will create a piece of art.

Saving an Ice Cube (with Civil Engineering)

In this interactive video, students will learn about the principles behind engineering and the core areas of civil engineering. They will have the opportunity to build something to save an ice cube and test it out!

Out of This World Chocolate Chip Cookies

Do you like baking? Are you interested in learning how to make the perfect chocolate chip cookies? “Out of This World Chocolate Chip Cookies” is a class that will teach you how to make chocolate chip cookies. It is brought to you by Zoe Gossa, Sofia Vergara, and Anezka Pospichalova who are all sophomores in the Academy of Culinary Arts and Hospitality Administration. The class will be taking place virtually from The Grill at BCA where ACAHA students take their classes. Along with baking cookies, you will learn basic kitchen safety as well as some classic terms used in the kitchen. Be sure to join the class because these cookies are guaranteed to be out of this world and take you to the moon!

Comics Creation

Learn to make a choose-your-own-adventure comic! In this class we'll learn about famous comic artists, walk through how to make the comic, from story boarding to adding drawings, and share your works with the original creator of the interactive template!

Business at War

"Business at War" revolves around the realm of business and delves into the significance of commerce and why it is so important to look into its connections with current events. The presentation will be focused around the Russian-Ukranian crisis, and the effects that it has had on the economy. The main discussion points of the lecture will be the concepts of sanctions and embargoes along with implications in the war, inflation, and supply chains. All of the general business concepts will be tied back to the discussion of current events to illustrate all of the concepts at play in real time.

Shaping the Stars!

Students will create stars, planets, and an astronaut in an online CAD website called OnShape. After these parts are created, students learn to assemble and render these parts to create a landscape in space!

Extracting DNA from Fruit (Set of 2 Courses)

"Berry" Special DNA

Have you ever wondered what DNA is? Where it is located? How important it is? In this class you will learn the basics of DNA and like in all sciences, you will later apply your knowledge. After briefly going over the value and importance of DNA, we will be going step by step with you on an experiment that extracts DNA from strawberries. By the end of the class, you will have a great foundation in the basics of DNA and have your very own DNA extract. All future scientists welcome! No prerequisite required.

The "Space" Inside You: Human DNA Lab + Microscopy

I'm sure everyone knows what DNA is, but have you ever seen your own DNA? Jump into the world of science and medicine with this hands-on experiment -- extract your DNA and learn more about it! All the materials used in this lab can be easily found around the house. Following the extraction of DNA will be an analysis of it under the microscope. Get a head start on your lab experience and the world of genetics!
Materials: a clean shot glass, dish soap, table salt, pineapple juice (or meat tenderizer, or cleaning solution for contact lenses), chilled alcohol (120 proof or higher), drinking straw (or pipette), toothpick
